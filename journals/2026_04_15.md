- # Optimal Control
	- A typical continuous time problem:
	- $$\begin{align}\min&\ h(\varphi(T;x_0,u)) + \int_0^Tg(\varphi(t;x_0,u), u(t))dt \\ \text{s.t. }& \varphi(T;x_0,u) \in \mathcal{T} \textit{ \tiny terminal constraints}\\ & (\varphi(t;x_0,u), u(t)) \in \mathcal{C}\ \forall t\in [0,T] \textit{ \tiny transient constraints}\end{align}$$
	-
	- Instead of first solving the dynamic (differential) equation and then optimize it, we can add the diff eq as a constraint
	- Bolza problems and Mayer problems??
	- The discrete version is
	- $$\begin{align}\min&\ h(x(K)) + \sum\limits_{k=0}^{K-1}g(x(k),u(k)) \\ \text{s.t. }& x(0) = x_0 \\& x(k+1) = f(x(k),u(k))\ \forall k\in \set{0,1,\cdots,K-1}\\& x(K) \in \mathcal{T} \textit{ \tiny terminal constraints}\\ & (x(k), u(k)) \in \mathcal{C}\ \forall k\in \set{0,1,\cdots,K-1} \textit{ \tiny transient constraints}\end{align}$$
	- > The running cost only goes upto $K-1$
